Silencing the Man, 03/26 –

This is an idea I had some years ago and I keep coming back to. Construction, especially in swiftly proliferating, highly urban, dense cities such as Seattle is a daily fixture of life. Cranes, construction zones, big cement mixer trucks are all widely seen… and heard! Construction is a highly noisy endeavor and there probably are public regulations regarding the times and places and noise levels which are appropriate. Still, wouldn’t it be great if all those sounds were better muffled? There may be a large untapped market in producing muffling materials and deployable surfaces for construction applications. Imagine coating a jackhammer with a composite that dampens sound? What if there were deployable sound proofing textiles that lined construction site walls and which decreased the sound intensity of things enclosed by them? I imagine there must already be work going into this... perhaps it is difficult and expensive to create such material that is both flexible, easily deployable, resistant, and inexpensive enough. Still, imagine the news that something like a completely silent or minimally noisy construction operation would garner! Any company offering such services at affordable prices would leave any competition out to hang.

Out of Key, 03/27 –

I forgot the key to my apartment… wouldn’t it be great if doorlocks had some kind of mechanically didactic component to them which completely obviated the use of a key? The key would be none other than a specific mechanical conformation on the face of the door (which you could set yourself). This could work very much like the way some smartphones secure their screen; i.e. patterns you can draw with your finger. This may be accomplished through a preset mechanical surface composed of rolling spheres and joints which turn and slide in response to pressure. This may be less practical than carrying keys if the puzzle is large and takes too long to input… however if enough complexity can be added to a small puzzle such that it is difficult to brute-force while still allowing dexterous development of muscle memory, then this may catch on! Learning it once might mean never forgetting keys again, barring any amnesia event. Plus since the "locking pattern" can be changed by the user, changing locks would not require buying a whole new set!

Nature's Requite, 03/29 –

There is a video circulating the web where some people in Liantang Village, Shenzen, China, are seen mesmerized, pointing smartphone cameras at something out of frame. The camera pans right and reveals a building. The camera pans upwards and reveals the building’s walls flush with a richly reddish-pink flower in bloom. It seems some kind of vine (maybe a Bougainvillea) made its way and covered the whole building; Leaving an incredibly pleasing view... a breath of fresh air amidst the drab monotony that characterizes cities. What if more buildings were covered by flowers/plants? Choosing the appropriate plants for the climate and architecture could be a stylistic and environmental choice. Buildings could be designed with these in mind, allowing for complex and beautiful growing patterns. Plus, if tenants or specialized companies do well in maintaining/scaping the greenery then windows and doors could remain functional, cleanly accentuated by natural foliage. I hope this is the future of cities, a revival of more natural historical roots, an embracing endemic flora in the name of aesthetics and environmental consciousness.
